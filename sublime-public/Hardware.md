The hardware plan is to empirically address plant care needs in order of grower relevancy with sensor and actuator devices.

The foremost challenge of a grower is often soil moisture, therefore the flagship device is a wireless **Volumetric Water Content (VWC)** / **Electrical Conductivity (EC)** / **temperature sensor.**  It provides reliable quantitative data about the water content, nutrient availability (EC)[^1], and temperature of its surrounding medium.

It is worth noting that while VWC tells you a content percentage of water in the surrounding medium. This water is not necessarily available to the plant. Soils with more clay for instance, can retain water yielding it unavailable to the plant. This is often a non-issue in controlled media growing environments, though it can be addressed by measuring Water Potential, which varies from a VWC sensor by reading not the content of the media, but how likely water is to move from one place to another. Water always moves from areas of _higher_ (less negative) water potential to _lower_ (more negative) water potential. As our goal is to address plant care needs in order of relevancy, and many growers use pre-amended soils we've decided to start with VWC.

The question of "which technique is best for determining VWC?" can be a can of worms.
